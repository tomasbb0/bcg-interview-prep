Recitals

WHEREAS:

A. The Company operates a healthtech business and seeks to expand its commercial operations through improved go-to-market capabilities;

B. The Contractor is an independent professional with expertise in commercial strategy, software development, and growth operations;

C. The Contractor operates as a self-employed professional (trabalhador independente) under Portuguese law and issues invoices via the Portuguese Tax Authority's Recibos Verdes system;

D. Both parties wish to establish a clear framework for the provision of services, compensation, and mutual obligations;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

Article 1: Nature of Relationship

1.1 Independent Contractor Status. The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor (prestataire de services / prestador de serviços) and not as an employee. There is no relationship of subordination (lien de subordination) between the parties.

1.2 No Employment Relationship. This Agreement does not create an employment contract (contrat de travail / contrato de trabalho). The Contractor:

  • Determines their own working hours and methods
  • Provides their own equipment and workspace
  • Is responsible for their own taxes, social security, and insurance
  • May provide services to other clients

1.3 EU Services Freedom. This Agreement is entered into under the freedom to provide services within the European Union, in accordance with Articles 56-62 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU).

Article 2: Scope of Services

2.1 Primary Services. The Contractor agrees to provide the following services:

A) GTM System Development

  • Custom Go-To-Market web application
  • AI-personalized bulk email campaign functionality
  • Lead import and management system
  • Sales pipeline visualization dashboard
  • Email open/click tracking and analytics
  • Campaign scheduling and management interface

B) Commercial Growth Services

  • Outbound lead generation and prospecting
  • Sales campaign design and execution
  • Pipeline management and optimization
  • Performance reporting

2.2 Minimum Viable Product (MVP). The GTM system MVP shall include the core features listed in 2.1(A) in functional, deployable form suitable for business operations.

2.3 Time Commitment. The Contractor commits to approximately fifteen to twenty (15-20) hours per week during the term of this Agreement.

2.4 Exclusions. Unless separately agreed in writing, Services do not include: hardware procurement, third-party software licenses, legal or accounting advice, or in-person attendance at Company premises.

Article 4: Invoicing and Tax Compliance

4.1 Invoicing. The Contractor shall issue invoices (Fatura-Recibo) via the Portuguese Tax Authority portal (Portal das Finanças) for all amounts due.

4.2 Invoice Requirements. Each invoice shall include:

  • Contractor's name, address, and Portuguese NIF
  • Company's name, address, and SIRET/registration number
  • Description of services rendered
  • Amount in EUR
  • VAT notation as per Article 4.3

4.3 VAT Treatment. As an intra-EU B2B service:

  • If Contractor is VAT-exempt (regime de isenção): Invoice states "IVA – regime de isenção, artigo 53.º do CIVA"
  • If reverse charge applies: Invoice states "IVA – autoliquidação / Reverse charge – Article 196, EU VAT Directive 2006/112/EC"

4.4 Tax Responsibility. The Contractor is solely responsible for:

  • Portuguese income tax (IRS) obligations
  • Portuguese social security (Segurança Social) contributions
  • Any applicable VAT obligations

4.5 No Withholding. The Company shall pay gross amounts without withholding, as no withholding tax applies to EU cross-border B2B services between Portugal and France.

Article 6: Advance Refund Conditions

6.1 Refund Triggers (Contractor Responsibility). The Contractor shall refund a proportionate amount of the Advance if:

  1. Contractor ceases communication for fourteen (14) or more consecutive days without valid reason;
  2. Contractor explicitly terminates before MVP delivery without mutual consent;
  3. Contractor fails to deliver MVP by deadline (excluding Company-caused delays).

6.2 Refund Calculation.

Refund Amount = Advance Payment − (Hours Worked × Hourly Rate)

Hours Worked shall be determined by the weekly progress reports under Article 5.2.

6.3 No Refund Conditions. The Contractor retains the full Advance with no refund obligation if:

  1. MVP is delivered as specified;
  2. Company terminates for reasons other than Contractor's material breach;
  3. Company fails to respond for fourteen (14) or more consecutive days;
  4. Company materially changes scope without written agreement;
  5. Mutual agreement to end after equivalent hours worked;
  6. Force majeure, illness, or emergency (with prompt communication).

6.4 Refund Timeline. Any refund due shall be paid within thirty (30) calendar days of the triggering event.

Article 7: Intellectual Property

7.1 Assignment. Upon full payment of all compensation due under this Agreement, all intellectual property rights in the GTM system and deliverables created under this Agreement shall transfer to the Company.

7.2 Pre-Existing IP. The Contractor retains all rights to pre-existing tools, frameworks, libraries, and methodologies. The Company receives a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use any pre-existing IP incorporated in the deliverables.

7.3 Interim License. Until full payment is received, the Company receives a limited, revocable license to use the deliverables for internal business purposes only.

Article 8: Confidentiality

8.1 Confidential Information. Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of proprietary information disclosed during the engagement, including: business strategies, customer data, technical specifications, pricing, and financial information.

8.2 Duration. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for two (2) years.

8.3 Permitted Disclosures. Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:

  1. Becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party;
  2. Was already known to the receiving party prior to disclosure;
  3. Is independently developed by the receiving party;
  4. Is required to be disclosed by law or court order.

Article 9: Good Faith and Dispute Resolution

9.1 Good Faith. Both parties commit to acting in good faith and communicating openly about any concerns or issues.

9.2 Notice Before Breach Claim. Neither party may claim material breach without first providing seven (7) days written notice specifying:

  • A reasonable opportunity to cure

Article 10: Term and Termination

10.1 Initial Term. This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for three (3) months.

10.2 Renewal. After the initial term, this Agreement renews month-to-month unless either party provides fourteen (14) days written notice of non-renewal.

10.3 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate with fourteen (14) days written notice, subject to the refund provisions in Article 6.

10.4 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate immediately upon material breach, following the notice procedure in Article 9.2.

10.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination:

  • All unpaid amounts become immediately due
  • Confidentiality obligations continue per Article 8.2
  • IP provisions apply per Article 7
