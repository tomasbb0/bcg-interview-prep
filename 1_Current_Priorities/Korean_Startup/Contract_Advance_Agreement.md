## Recitals

WHEREAS, Company desires to engage Contractor to develop a Go-To-Market (GTM) system and provide commercial growth services;

WHEREAS, Contractor has represented that they possess the skills and experience necessary to perform such services;

WHEREAS, both parties wish to establish a clear framework for compensation, deliverables, and mutual obligations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements hereinafter set forth, the parties agree as follows:

---

## Article 1: Scope of Services

1.1. **Services.** Contractor agrees to provide the following services ("Services"):

   a) Development of a custom Go-To-Market (GTM) web application including:
      - Bulk AI-personalized email campaign functionality
      - Lead import and management system
      - Sales pipeline visualization dashboard
      - Email tracking and analytics
      - Campaign management interface

   b) Commercial growth activities including:
      - Outbound lead generation
      - Sales campaign execution
      - Pipeline management

1.2. **Minimum Viable Product (MVP).** The GTM system MVP shall include the core features listed in 1.1(a) in functional form, suitable for business use.

1.3. **Time Commitment.** Contractor commits to providing approximately fifteen to twenty (15-20) hours of work per week during the term of this Agreement.

## Article 3: Deliverables and Timeline

3.1. **MVP Delivery.** Contractor shall deliver the GTM System MVP within six (6) weeks of receiving the Advance Payment, specifically by:

   **MVP Deadline:** ______________________________________ (Date)

3.2. **Progress Updates.** Contractor shall provide Company with weekly written progress updates, including:
   - Hours worked during the period
   - Tasks completed
   - Tasks planned for next period
   - Any blockers or concerns

3.3. **Acceptance.** Company shall have seven (7) days following MVP delivery to review and request reasonable modifications. MVP shall be deemed accepted if no written objections are received within this period.

---

## Article 4: Advance Refund Conditions

4.1. **Contractor-Triggered Refund.** Contractor shall refund a portion of the Advance if:

   a) Contractor ceases communication for fourteen (14) or more consecutive days without prior notice or valid reason;
   
   b) Contractor explicitly terminates this Agreement before MVP delivery without mutual consent;
   
   c) Contractor fails to deliver MVP by the deadline specified in Article 3.1, extended by any delays caused by Company.

4.2. **Refund Calculation.** Refund amount shall be calculated as:

   ```
   Refund = Advance - (Hours Worked × Hourly Rate)
   ```

   Where Hours Worked is determined by the weekly progress updates provided under Article 3.2.

4.3. **No Refund Conditions.** Contractor shall retain the full Advance with no refund obligation if:

   a) Contractor delivers the MVP as described in Article 1;
   
   b) Company terminates this Agreement for reasons other than Contractor's material breach;
   
   c) Company fails to respond to Contractor's communications for fourteen (14) or more consecutive days;
   
   d) Company materially changes the scope of Services without mutual written agreement;
   
   e) Both parties mutually agree to end the engagement after Contractor has worked the equivalent hours;
   
   f) External circumstances beyond Contractor's control (illness, emergency, force majeure) prevent delivery, provided Contractor communicates promptly.

4.4. **Refund Timeline.** Any refund due shall be paid within thirty (30) days of the triggering event.

---

## Article 5: Intellectual Property

5.1. **Work Product.** Upon full payment of all compensation due, all intellectual property rights in the GTM system and deliverables shall transfer to Company.

5.2. **Contractor Tools.** Contractor retains rights to any pre-existing tools, frameworks, or methodologies used in creating the deliverables.

5.3. **License.** Until full payment is received, Company receives a limited license to use the deliverables for internal business purposes only.

---

## Article 6: Confidentiality

6.1. **Confidential Information.** Both parties agree to keep confidential any proprietary information disclosed during the engagement, including but not limited to: business strategies, customer lists, technical specifications, and financial information.

6.2. **Duration.** Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years.

6.3. **Exceptions.** Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was already known to the receiving party; (c) is required to be disclosed by law.

---

## Article 7: Good Faith and Dispute Resolution

7.1. **Good Faith.** Both parties agree to act in good faith throughout the term of this Agreement and to communicate openly about any concerns.

7.2. **Notice Before Breach Claim.** Neither party may claim material breach without first providing seven (7) days written notice specifying the alleged breach and opportunity to cure.

7.3. **Direct Resolution.** Any dispute shall first be addressed through direct discussion between the parties for a period of fourteen (14) days.

7.4. **Mediation.** If direct resolution fails, parties agree to attempt mediation before pursuing legal action.

7.5. **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of ______________________________________ (Country/Jurisdiction).

---

## Article 8: Term and Termination

8.1. **Initial Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of three (3) months.

8.2. **Renewal.** Following the initial term, this Agreement shall renew on a month-to-month basis unless either party provides fourteen (14) days written notice of termination.

8.3. **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason with fourteen (14) days written notice, subject to the refund provisions in Article 4.

8.4. **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate immediately upon material breach by the other party, following the notice procedure in Article 7.2.

---

## Article 9: General Provisions

9.1. **Independent Contractor.** Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee of Company. Contractor is responsible for their own taxes and insurance.

9.2. **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements.

9.3. **Amendments.** Any amendments to this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both parties.

9.4. **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full effect.

9.5. **Notices.** All notices shall be in writing and sent to the email addresses specified above.
