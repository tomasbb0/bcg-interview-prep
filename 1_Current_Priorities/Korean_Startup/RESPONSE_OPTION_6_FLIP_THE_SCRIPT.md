# Response Option #6: Flip The Script 🔄
## "Help ME with MY Mental Health Startup"

---

## THE STRATEGY

Instead of waiting for his decision, you **take the lead**. You're starting your own mental health startup and inviting HIM to help YOU - passively for now, more actively once he's done with his hackathon.

**Why this works:**
1. **Reframes power dynamic** - You're not the candidate waiting; you're the founder inviting
2. **Removes pressure** - He can say yes to something easy (passive advice)
3. **Creates curiosity** - What's Tomas building? He'll want to know
4. **Keeps door open** - Still allows future co-founder exploration
5. **Shows you move forward** - You're not waiting around for anyone

---

## THE MESSAGE

### Option A: Warm + Exciting
```
Hey Young! 

No worries at all about the hackathon - that sounds like a great opportunity. Good luck with it!

Actually, I've been doing some thinking on my end too. I'm exploring launching my own venture in the mental health / behavioral change space. Given your experience in healthtech and Korea, I'd love to get your perspective sometimes - nothing heavy, just the occasional async question when something comes up.

Once you're done with the hackathon, maybe we can compare notes? Could be interesting to see if there's any overlap, or just learn from each other's approaches.

Either way, rooting for you at DeepMind! 🙌
```

### Option B: More Direct + Specific
```
Hey Young,

Good luck with the hackathon! DeepMind events are always intense.

Quick thought: I'm actually spinning up something in the mental health space myself - focused on [X specific angle]. Given your healthtech background, would you be open to being a sounding board occasionally? Super passive - just the odd question via text, no meetings or commitments.

Once you're through February, we could explore if there's any synergy between what you're building and what I'm working on. Either collaboration or just learning from each other.

Let me know if you're open to that light-touch connection for now. 🤝
```

### Option C: Maximum Intrigue
```
Young,

All good on timing - hackathons wait for no one! 

Actually, perfect timing in a way. I've been deep in something that I think might interest you. Mental health space, but a different angle than what we discussed for your venture.

Would love to bounce a few ideas off you when you have bandwidth. Nothing urgent - just async, low-key. Think of it as being an informal advisor while you're heads-down on other things.

Good luck crushing it 🔥
```

## EXPECTED REACTIONS BY PERSONALITY

### 🥇 Careful Committer
- **Initial:** Surprised, intrigued
- **Likely response:** "That sounds interesting, happy to help occasionally"
- **Outcome:** Stays engaged, might accelerate his decision on collaboration

### 🥇 Family First
- **Initial:** Relieved (no pressure) + curious
- **Likely response:** "Sure, feel free to ping me"
- **Outcome:** Easy yes, low commitment matches his bandwidth

### 🥈 Always Comparing
- **Initial:** Very intrigued - now he's comparing TWO opportunities
- **Likely response:** Lots of questions about what you're building
- **Outcome:** FOMO kicks in, he doesn't want to miss out

### 🥉 Polite Avoider
- **Initial:** Easy out if he wants, but...
- **Likely response:** Either accepts (it's so low-ask) or goes silent
- **Outcome:** Either way, you've moved on with dignity

## FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOL

**If he says yes to being sounding board:**
- Wait 3-5 days
- Send ONE specific question (not generic)
- Example: "Quick q: In Korea, how do people typically discover mental health apps? Through doctors, ads, or word of mouth?"
- His engagement level tells you everything

**If he goes silent:**
- You've already moved on
- No follow-up needed
- You're building YOUR thing

**If he asks "tell me more about what you're building":**
- Great sign! He's curious
- Share just enough to intrigue
- Leave room for a proper conversation in Feb
