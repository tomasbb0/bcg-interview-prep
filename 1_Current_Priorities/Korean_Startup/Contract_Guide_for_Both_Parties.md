## 1. Summary: What This Contract Does

This contract sets up a working relationship where:

- **Tomás** builds a sales automation system and helps grow the business
- **Young-Wook's company** pays Tomás for this work

### What Tomás Gets

| Protection | How the Contract Does It |
|:-----------|:-------------------------|
| Gets paid upfront so he can start working | Advance payment clause |
| Keeps the advance if Young-Wook cancels | No-refund conditions |
| Keeps the advance if Young-Wook disappears | 14-day silence clause |
| Clear rules before anyone can claim "breach" | 7-day notice requirement |
| Can't be treated like an employee | Independent contractor clause |

### What Young-Wook Gets

| Protection | How the Contract Does It |
|:-----------|:-------------------------|
| Gets money back if Tomás disappears | Refund clause with 14-day trigger |
| Gets money back if Tomás doesn't deliver | MVP deadline with refund |
| Knows exactly what Tomás will build | Detailed scope of work |
| Sees progress every week | Weekly update requirement |
| Owns the final product | IP transfer upon payment |

### The Core Deal in One Sentence

> Young-Wook pays Tomás upfront. Tomás builds the system. If Tomás delivers, he keeps the money. If Tomás disappears, Young-Wook gets it back. If Young-Wook cancels, Tomás keeps it.

## 2. Article 1: Nature of Relationship

### What It Says

Tomás is an independent contractor, not an employee.

### What This Actually Means

- Tomás chooses his own hours and how he works
- Tomás pays his own taxes in Portugal
- Tomás can work for other clients
- Young-Wook doesn't provide benefits, holiday pay, or equipment
- This is a business-to-business relationship

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Freedom to work his own way | No employment obligations |
| Can have other clients | No payroll taxes to pay |
| Controls his schedule | Simpler legal relationship |

## 4. Article 3: Compensation

### What It Says

Young-Wook pays:
- **Advance** (upfront): Agreed amount
- **Tool budget**: €150 for software costs
- **Monthly retainer**: Agreed amount each month
- **Commission**: Percentage of closed deals
- **Running costs**: ~€50/month for hosting and services

### What This Actually Means

The advance is not a gift. It's pre-payment for about 60 hours of work. If Tomás works 30 hours and leaves, he refunds half.

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Gets money to start working immediately | Clear what he's paying |
| Running costs covered | Advance tied to real work |
| Commission rewards success | Can calculate what hours cost |

## 6. Article 5: Deliverables and Timeline

### What It Says

- Tomás has 6 weeks from receiving payment to deliver the MVP
- Tomás sends weekly progress updates
- Young-Wook has 7 days to review and accept

### What This Actually Means

The clock starts when the money arrives, not when the contract is signed. Weekly updates create a paper trail showing work is happening.

If Young-Wook doesn't respond within 7 days of delivery, the system is automatically considered accepted.

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Deadline tied to when payment arrives | Gets regular progress updates |
| 7-day auto-acceptance protects against silence | Can raise issues early |
| Weekly updates prove work happened | Clear delivery expectations |

## 7. Article 6: Advance Refund Conditions

### This Is the Most Important Section

### When Tomás Must Refund (Tomás's Responsibility)

| What Happens | Consequence |
|:-------------|:------------|
| Tomás goes silent for 14+ days without reason | Refund unused portion |
| Tomás quits before finishing | Refund unused portion |
| Tomás misses the deadline (his fault) | Refund unused portion |

**How the refund is calculated:**

```
Refund = Advance − (Hours Worked × Hourly Rate)
```

*Example: If advance was €1,000 for 60 hours (€17/hour), and Tomás worked 40 hours:*
- *Tomás keeps: 40 × €17 = €680*
- *Tomás refunds: €320*

### When Tomás Keeps Everything (Not Tomás's Fault)

| What Happens | Tomás Keeps Advance? |
|:-------------|:---------------------|
| Tomás delivers the MVP | ✓ Yes |
| Young-Wook cancels the project | ✓ Yes |
| Young-Wook goes silent for 14+ days | ✓ Yes |
| Young-Wook changes the scope without agreement | ✓ Yes |
| Both agree to stop after the hours were worked | ✓ Yes |
| Tomás gets sick but communicates about it | ✓ Yes |

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Protected from random "you failed" claims | Gets money back if Tomás truly bails |
| Keeps money if Young-Wook causes problems | Fair calculation for refunds |
| 14 days = reasonable buffer, not instant | Clear triggers, no ambiguity |

## 8. Article 7: Intellectual Property

### What It Says

When Young-Wook finishes paying, he owns the GTM system completely. Until then, Tomás owns it.

### What This Actually Means

- After full payment: Young-Wook owns everything Tomás built
- Before full payment: Tomás keeps ownership (this protects against non-payment)
- Tomás keeps rights to his own pre-existing tools and methods

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Keeps leverage if not paid | Owns what he pays for |
| Can reuse his own frameworks elsewhere | Full rights to modify and sell |

## 10. Article 9: Good Faith and Disputes

### What It Says

Before anyone claims "breach of contract":
1. Must give 7 days written notice
2. Must explain what the problem is
3. Must give a chance to fix it

If there's a dispute:
1. First: Talk directly (14 days)
2. Then: Mediation (neutral third party)
3. Last resort: Court

### What This Actually Means

Neither party can suddenly say "you broke the contract" without warning. Both must try to solve problems before going to lawyers.

### Why This Clause Exists

| For Tomás | For Young-Wook |
|:----------|:---------------|
| Can't be ambushed with breach claims | Can't be ambushed with breach claims |
| 7 days to fix any problems | 7 days to fix any problems |
| Saves legal costs | Saves legal costs |

**This protects both parties equally.**

### Why This Clause Exists

## Final Notes

This contract is designed to be **fair to both parties**:

- Young-Wook is protected from Tomás disappearing with his money
- Tomás is protected from Young-Wook randomly claiming failure
- Clear deliverables, clear timelines, clear money
- Both must communicate before claiming breach
- Both can exit with proper notice

**It's not one-sided. It's professional. It builds trust.**
