# SECTION 1: Predicted Q&A with Young-Wook

## 1.1 His Current Situation

| Your Question | Predicted Answer | What This Means |
|:--------------|:-----------------|:----------------|
| "How are things going with sales right now?" | "It's okay, we have 5 clients, but all through word of mouth. I need to scale but I don't have time." | **He knows he has a problem.** No outbound = single point of failure. |
| "What does your typical week look like for finding new customers?" | "Honestly, not much. I'm focused on product and the existing clients. When I do outreach, it takes forever." | **He's not doing outbound.** This is your opening. |
| "How are you currently reaching out to potential clients?" | "Mostly introductions from existing clients. I built a small tool to find contact info but it's basic." | **Word of mouth isn't scalable.** His tool is 1% of what you offer. |
| "What does your tool do exactly?" | "You type a name, it searches Google APIs and finds emails and info about that person." | **This is lead LOOKUP, not lead GENERATION.** Huge difference. |

---

## 1.2 Surfacing the Pain

| Your Question | Predicted Answer | What This Means |
|:--------------|:-----------------|:----------------|
| "What's the most frustrating part of sales for you?" | "The time. I have to do everything manually. Write each email. Follow up. Track who replied." | **He's drowning in manual work.** Your system fixes all of this. |
| "How much time do you spend on outreach each week?" | "When I actually do it? Maybe 5-10 hours. But honestly I skip it most weeks." | **Inconsistent effort = inconsistent results.** Automation solves this. |
| "Out of the outreach you do, how many convert?" | "Not many. Maybe 1-2% reply, and closing takes months." | **Low conversion + long cycles.** Personalization + follow-up = your fix. |
| "What happens to leads that don't respond the first time?" | "Nothing. I don't have time to chase them." | **He's leaving money on the table.** Follow-up sequences = your system. |

---

## 1.3 Comparing His Tool to Yours

**KEY MOMENT:** Don't trash his tool. Respect it. Then show the gap.

| Your Question | Predicted Answer | Your Follow-Up |
|:--------------|:-----------------|:---------------|
| "That tool you built — does it help you send emails, or just find them?" | "Just find them. I still have to write and send everything myself." | "So it's step 1 of 10. What about the other 9?" |
| "Does it track who opened your emails?" | "No." | "So you don't know who's warm?" |
| "Does it automatically follow up if someone doesn't reply?" | "No, I have to do that manually." | "That's where 80% of deals are won." |
| "Does it show you a pipeline — who's at what stage?" | "No, I use a spreadsheet." | "What if it all lived in one place?" |

**Then say:**

> "Look, what you built is smart. It solves the 'find the email' problem. But finding the email is 10% of the work. The other 90% — personalization, sending at scale, follow-ups, tracking, pipeline — that's what kills your time. That's what I've built."

# SECTION 2: Making Him See the Value

## 2.1 Anchor to His Numbers

| Your Question | Predicted Answer | What You're Setting Up |
|:--------------|:-----------------|:-----------------------|
| "What's your average deal size?" | "$30-50K" | That's €28-46K per client |
| "How many new clients do you need this year?" | "3-5 would be great" | 3 × €35K = €105K potential |
| "If this system helps you close even ONE extra client, what's that worth?" | "$30-50K" | Your total cost to him = maybe €15K/year. ROI = 200-300% |

**Say:**

> "So we're talking about a system that costs you maybe €12-15K over the year — and if it lands you just ONE extra hospital, you've made 3x your money back. And that's the floor."

---

## 2.2 Compare to Alternatives

| Your Question | Predicted Answer | Your Point |
|:--------------|:-----------------|:-----------|
| "Have you thought about hiring a salesperson?" | "Yes, but they're expensive and I'd have to train them." | "A junior SDR in France costs €40-50K/year. And they need tools anyway." |
| "What about outsourcing to a sales agency?" | "I looked into it. €3-5K/month minimum." | "And they don't build you a system you own. I do." |
| "Your time — if you're spending 10 hours/week on outreach, what's that worth?" | "A lot. I should be on product." | "At even €50/hour, that's €2,000/month of your time. I cost half that." |

---

## 2.3 Position Your Offer

**Don't say "I'm cheap." Say "I'm the smart choice."**

> "Here's what I'm offering:
> 
> 1. A complete GTM system — not just email lookup, but the whole pipeline
> 2. AI-personalized outreach at scale
> 3. Automated follow-ups so nothing slips
> 4. A dashboard so you know exactly what's working
> 5. Me running the first campaigns with you
> 
> Compared to hiring someone or using an agency, you get the system AND the operator for a fraction of the cost. And after the MVP, you OWN the system."

---

## 2.4 Handling the Advance Request

**He might push back on paying upfront. Here's how to handle:**

| His Objection | Your Response |
|:--------------|:--------------|
| "Why do I need to pay an advance?" | "It covers the first 60 hours of development and tools. I'm essentially pre-selling you time at a discount." |
| "Can I just pay monthly?" | "The advance is how I fund the tool budget and my dev time before the system is live. Once the MVP is done, it's just the retainer." |
| "That's a lot upfront." | "It's less than 2% of what one new client is worth to you. And there's a refund clause if I don't deliver." |
| "I need to think about it." | "Totally fair. What would you need to know to feel confident?" |

---

## 2.5 Handling "Your System Is Like What I Built"

**He might compare your system to his. Don't argue. Expand.**

> "What you built is great for step 1 — finding contact info. But here's what happens after that:
>
> 1. You have to write each email manually
> 2. You have to send them one by one
> 3. You have to remember to follow up
> 4. You have to track who replied in a spreadsheet
> 5. You have no idea who opened what
>
> My system does steps 2-10 automatically. So you're comparing a magnifying glass to a telescope."

---

## 2.6 The Close

Don't push. Let him arrive:

> "Based on everything you've said — the time you're losing, the leads that fall through, the deals you're not closing — does this feel like a good investment?"

If yes:

> "Great. I've got a simple contract ready. We can sign today and I'll have the MVP in 6 weeks. What would you need from me to feel good moving forward?"

If hesitation:

> "What's the one thing holding you back?"

# What NOT to Do

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You |
|:--------|:-----------------|
| Trash-talk his tool | He built it himself. He's proud. Respect it, then expand. |
| Lead with your features | Nobody cares about features. They care about outcomes. |
| Justify your price | Your price is justified by HIS math, not your explanations. |
| Discount immediately | If you go €1,000 → €700 fast, he'll think €700 is the real price. |
| Talk more than him | You're not pitching. You're diagnosing. 70% listening. |
| Mention you need money | Never. You're offering value, not begging. |
