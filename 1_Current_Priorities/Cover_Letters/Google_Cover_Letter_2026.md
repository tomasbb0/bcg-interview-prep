# Cover Letter - Google Account Executive, New Business (February 2026)

---

Dear Hiring Team,

My name is Tomás, I am 26 years old, and I want to come back to Google. Not because it sounds good on paper, but because after spending the past year and a half building a company from zero, I now know exactly what kind of seller I am — and Google is where I want to do it.

Since leaving my internship at Google LCS in August 2024, I co-founded an AI startup called Pairwire and ran the entire commercial operation: prospecting, outreach, demos, negotiation, and close. No sales team, no brand recognition, no playbook. I built a €147K pipeline of contracted enterprise pilots, closed 23 engagements including Fortune 500 firms, and designed an outbound engine that processed over 7,000 leads. I learned what it actually takes to get a stranger to pick up the phone, trust you, and sign a contract — and I got comfortable with the 99 rejections that come before each yes.

That startup experience changed me. During my Google internship, I knew I loved the work — presenting to 237 senior clients at the flagship partner event, building dashboards my LCS teammates actually adopted, reaching out to Googlers around the world for benchmarking. But I was still a student with good instincts. Now I am a seller with real scars. I know how to qualify, how to handle objections, how to manage a pipeline end-to-end, and how to stay disciplined when deals slip. Those are things I could only learn by doing them without a safety net.

Before Google and Pairwire, I spent seven months at Amazon leading the B2B expansion into Portugal — managing 2,311 accounts, outperforming full-time colleagues, and learning to create demand in a market where none existed. At EY, I learned that thoroughness matters as much as speed. Each of these roles taught me something different, but they all pointed in the same direction: I want to sell products I believe in, to clients who need them, at a company that moves fast and thinks big.

I hold an MSc in Strategic Management from Rotterdam School of Management and a BSc from Nova School of Business and Economics, with a semester at the National University of Singapore. These gave me the analytical foundation, but the real education has been commercial.

I left Google once to go build something. I did. Now I am ready to bring everything I learned back.

Tomás Batalha
