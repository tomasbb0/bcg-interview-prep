SIMPLE SNACK BAR
Case question
Your friend has created a new healthy snack bar that is free of all 14 recognized allergens, including gluten, milk, and nuts. 
It tastes great and is completely unique in the oversaturated snack market. Your friend needs help with determining the 
right price for the snack bar. 
How would you approach this problem?

SPLASH PARK
Possible answer
Increase revenue
1
a.
Increase number of clients
•
Grow capacity (e.g. opening hours, new rides)
•
Grow demand (e.g. promotions, new sales channels, 
loyalty scheme) 
b.
Increase spend per client
•
Increase prices
•
Create add-on services (e.g. VIP pass, restaurants, massage)
Decrease costs
2
a.
Decrease service level (e.g. close rides, opening hours, maintenance, 
water temperature, life guards)
b.
Do the same with less (e.g. identify waste)
c.
Reduce input costs (e.g. cut wages, renegotiate with suppliers)
Why this structure?
This structure starts with a standard 
proﬁtability framework.
It identiﬁes that volume is a 
supply / demand issue.
The third-level drivers are highly 
tailored to the nature of the business. 
SUNSHINE VACATIONS
Case question
A British package holiday provider has seen its bookings decline by 10% this year due to an abnormally hot summer in the 
UK. Rather than pursue the sunshine abroad, British holidaymakers chose to stay in the UK. This has resulted in a decline 
in reported proﬁt, and the company’s share price has dropped throughout the year. The CEO has asked for your help in 
turning the situation around for next year, whether there’s rain or sunshine in the UK.
How would you approach this problem?

SUNSHINE VACATIONS
Possible answer
Focus on most proﬁtable offers
1
a.
Identify and decrease capacity of least-proﬁtable packages
b.
Identify and increase capacity of most-proﬁtable packages
Increase proﬁtability across all our packages
2
a.
Increase demand
b.
Review our pricing
c.
Decrease our costs
i.
Overhead
ii.
Cost of packages (e.g. remove services, renegotiate contracts, 
change or consolidate suppliers)
Identify and invest in proﬁtable growth opportunities
3
a.
UK packages?
b.
Lower cost destination?
c.
Cruises?
d.
Specialized holidays (e.g. adventures, sports, identity-focus)?
Why this structure?
Because Sunshine sells a variety of 
products, its proﬁtability will be 
driven by the mix of products sold.
Therefore, instead of using the 
proﬁtability framework, we start by 
looking into selling less of the 
least-proﬁtable products and 
vice-versa. 
We then move on to other 
proﬁtability drivers (i.e. price, cost).
Finally, we explore new ideas for 
attractive and proﬁtable products.

happen.

‘BRISTOL VALLEY’
Possible answer
Identify target technologies
1
a.
Emerging trends
b.
Strengths of local universities
c.
Strengths of employers
Strengthen the ecosystem
2
a.
Universities - increase research funding and students 
b.
Employers - locate R&D centers; collaborate with universities
c.
Funding - create dedicated venture funds; organize investors’ 
network and conferences
d.
Entrepreneurs - encourage technology transfers; offer free 
incubation and training; offer free workspace
Improve location attractiveness (e.g. transport, accommodation, upgrade 
city center area with investment)
3
Why this structure?
We start by deﬁning the tech ﬁelds on 
which Bristol should focus - since this 
has not been deﬁned in the brief.
We then look at the key components 
of the ecosystem. To ﬁnd what these 
components could be, we might use 
the Silicon Valley as an example. 
Finally, a key reason for the Silicon 
Valley’s success is the attractiveness 
of the region, so that should be 
considered as well.
SOCIAL RETURN CAPITAL
Case question
In April 2019, the Australian Prime Minister launched Social Return Capital, the world’s ﬁrst ever social investment 
market builder. The goal of the organization is to improve access to capital for social enterprises. Social investments are 
driven by a social purpose, rather than the sole pursuit of proﬁt. Today, social investments represent a very small share of 
assets under professional management in Australia.
You’ve been asked to investigate why Social Return Capital hasn't received more investment so far. What factors would 
you look into?

SOCIAL RETURN CAPITAL
Possible answer
Lack of supply
1
a.
Limited need of capital from social enterprises 
b.
Not enough social enterprises to invest in
c.
Lack of asset managers and investment vehicles in this asset class
Lack of demand
2
a.
Investors don’t know about social investments
b.
Investors don’t care enough about social return
c.
Financial or social return have been insufﬁcient
d.
Unfavorable tax treatment
Lack of intermediation
3
a.
Intermediaries don’t distribute these products
b.
Intermediaries don’t know how to sell these products
c.
Intermediaries don’t promote these products
Why this structure?
This structure is a root cause analysis 
that leverages a supply / demand 
framework, which is a good way to 
look at markets in general. 
The addition of the intermediation 
driver is important, as it’s a plausible 
cause of the lack of uptake of these 
products.

The CEO has asked for your help in identifying a solution.
How would you analyze this problem?

Quality of applications

Our process
2

Burdensome process is leading to abandoned applications or

Our approval rules 
3
Competitiveness of our offers
4

Rates not competitive
b.
Features not attractive

of each possible root cause of the 
decline in mortgage completion.

application would follow, from 
creation to completion. 
It shows a good understanding of how 
loans are extended to consumers.
