# THE STARTUP PLAYBOOK: Lessons for the Next Venture

You paid a high price (10 months of work + €37k in lost wages) for this education. **Do not waste it.** Here are the rules for your next startup.

## 1. The "Paper First" Rule
*   **Never work a single day without a signed contract.**
*   **"We'll figure out equity later" = "You are getting 0%."**
*   **The Fix:** Use a standard "FAST Agreement" (Founder Advisor Standard Template) or a simple Consulting Agreement with *explicit* vesting and equity numbers from Day 1.

## 2. The "Vesting" Trap
*   **What happened:** You worked for 10 months but had no vesting schedule, so they tried to claim you owned nothing.
*   **The Fix:** Standard 4-year vesting with a **1-year cliff**.
    *   *Crucial:* Ensure the "Cliff" starts from your **start date**, not the "signing date." (This was the Pairwire trap).

## 3. The "Title" Defense
*   **What happened:** They tried to label you a "Consultant" to deny your Co-Founder status.
*   **The Fix:** Your contract must explicitly state your title is "Co-Founder."
*   **The "Public Proof" Rule:** Ensure you are listed on the website and LinkedIn as Co-Founder immediately. If they hesitate, it's a red flag.

## 4. The "IP Assignment" Lever
*   **What happened:** You built IP (brand, sales process) without a contract. This gave you leverage because you technically still owned it.
*   **The Fix (as a Founder):** When *you* hire people, make them sign IP assignment agreements immediately.
*   **The Fix (as an Employee):** Never assign your IP until you have your equity grant. Your IP is your only collateral.

## 5. The "Bad Cop" Lawyer
*   **Lesson:** You don't need to be the jerk. Hire a lawyer (or use ChatGPT/Agent) to be the "Bad Cop."
*   *Phrase:* "My lawyer advises me I can't sign this." It removes emotion and makes you look professional.

## 6. Red Flags to Run From
*   Founders who say "contracts are just formalities."
*   Founders who delay signing for months.
*   Founders who try to pay you below minimum wage "for tax reasons."
*   Founders who get angry when you ask for things in writing.
