## 1. The Mindset Shift
**You must stop thinking like an employee who was fired.**
*   **Employees** get fired for underperformance.
*   **Co-Founders** have "breakups" or "exits" due to strategic misalignment.

**Your Reality:** You worked for 10 months, largely unpaid, building the company's foundation. When the time came to sign the final shareholder agreement, the terms changed (the equity dispute), so you walked away. **That is a position of strength, not weakness.**

## 2. The Interview Narrative
When asked "Why did you leave Pairwire?" or "What happened?", do **not** say "it didn't work out" or "we fought."

**Say this:**
> "I joined Pairwire as a late Co-Founder to build their commercial and operational infrastructure from scratch. Over 10 months, I built their entire sales pipeline, managed key pilot negotiations with enterprise clients like Enghouse and Mazars, and established their brand identity.
>
> However, as we approached the formalization of the shareholder agreement, we realized we had different visions for the company's long-term equity structure. Since we couldn't align on the partnership terms, I decided it was the right time to exit and apply those 0he-to-1 building skills to a new challenge where incentives are fully aligned."

**Why this works:**
*   It highlights **achievements** (Pipeline, Pilots, Brand).
*   It frames the exit as a **business decision** (Equity structure), not a performance issue.
*   It shows **maturity** (Knowing when to walk away).

## 4. The Reference Trap
The biggest risk is a future employer calling Martim and him saying: "Tomás is lying, he was never a Co-Founder."

**How to neutralize this risk:**

### A. The "Pre-Emptive Strike" (In the Interview)
If you think they might call him, you must control the narrative **before** they make the call.
*   **Say this:** "I want to be transparent: my exit involved a dispute over equity ownership. Because we couldn't agree on the final terms, the relationship ended on strained terms. I would not rely on him for an objective reference right now. However, I can provide **documentation** (emails, government registration) that confirms my role and title during my tenure."

### B. The "Proof" Defense
If an employer comes back and says "Martim said you weren't a Co-Founder," you calmly reply:
*   "I understand he might say that now due to our dispute. However, here is the **email to Enghouse** where I signed as Co-Founder with him in CC. Here is the **IAPMEI registration** where the company listed me as CSMO. I operated with this title for 10 months with his full approval."
*   *This makes Martim look like the liar/unstable one, not you.*

You tell Martim:

**Why this fails for him:**
1.  **Digital Forensics:** You have the original PDF file (`00003035...`) sent via WhatsApp on Feb 3rd. The file metadata (creation date) and the WhatsApp server logs prove *that* specific file (the blank one) was the one delivered.
2.  **The "Lock-In" Tactic:** Before you reveal you know it's blank, you force him to commit to the Feb 3rd version.

**The Script to Trap Him:**
> "Martim, just to be clear on the paperwork—the PDF you sent me on WhatsApp on February 3rd, titled 'Pairwire, Inc. - Consulting Agreement - Tomas Batalha.pdf', that was the final version intended for signature, correct? I just want to make sure I'm looking at the right document."

**Crucial Rule:** **NEVER** delete the original PDF from your phone or computer. It is your proof of his incompetence.

## 7. The No Contract Advantage
You asked: *"Am I not less protected if he says I was not under a contract?"*
The answer is **NO**. You are actually **safer** and have **more leverage** without this specific contract. Here is why:

### A. What the "Contract" Gave You (The Bad Deal)
If that PDF were valid, you would have:
1.  **Zero Pay:** It explicitly listed Compensation as `[N/A]`.
2.  **Zero Equity:** It explicitly listed Shares as `[ ]` (empty).
3.  **IP Assignment:** You would have legally given away ownership of every file, design, and lead you created.
4.  **Non-Compete:** You would be banned from working in the industry.

### B. What "No Contract" Gives You (The Power Position)
If that PDF is void (because it's incomplete/unsigned), the default laws apply:
1.  **IP Ownership:** In many jurisdictions, if a consultant is not paid and has no written contract assigning IP, **the consultant retains ownership of their work.**
    *   *Leverage:* "Martim, if we don't have a contract, then technically I still own the Brand Book, the Sales Pipeline, and the Strategy Decks. If you want to keep using them, we need to sign a settlement agreement (the reference letter)."
2.  **No Non-Compete:** You are free to work for anyone immediately.
3.  **Misclassification Risk for Him:** Without a B2B contract, labor authorities might view you as a "de facto employee," which exposes *him* to huge fines for unpaid social security and taxes. He *needs* a contract to protect himself from the government. You don't.

**Verdict:** The "Contract" was a pair of handcuffs. "No Contract" is freedom + leverage.

## 9. Immediate Action Plan (Answering Your Panic)

You asked: *"Is it official already? Should I stop working? Should I start my own startup RIGHT NOW?"*

### A. Is the Contract "Official" without a signature?
**NO.** Martim is bluffing or legally illiterate.
*   **IP Assignment:** In almost all jurisdictions, transferring Intellectual Property (Copyright) requires a **written, signed instrument**. If you didn't sign it, you likely still own your work.
*   **Non-Compete:** A non-compete generally requires a **signed agreement** AND **consideration** (payment). Since the contract says `[N/A]` for payment and you didn't sign it, the non-compete is almost certainly **void**.
*   *Conclusion:* You are not bound by the restrictions. You are free.

### B. Resume Status: "Present" or "Past"?
