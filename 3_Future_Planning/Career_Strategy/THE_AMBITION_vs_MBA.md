## Insights from Ex-BCGer (João) — December 2024

### The 1-5 Framework (Revolut Guy)

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Arrives, understands objectives, figures out how to get there, executes. Full autonomy. |
| **4** | You explain objectives + how to get there, they execute well. |
| **3** | You explain objectives + path, they follow. |
| **2-1** | Need heavy guidance. |

**João's insight:** *"At BCG, it doesn't matter if you're a 3 or a 5. Being a 5 is NOT rewarded in bonuses or recognition. If you consider yourself exceptional in terms of impact and purpose, BCG is not the place to feel like a 5."*

---

### What BCG Actually Gives You

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Exposure to senior, important people | Working 16h/day, 6 days/week |
| Learn how to run a company | Skills valued: slides, structured models — not creativity |
| BCG stamp accelerates sales | Lower ceiling long-term |
| Network of well-positioned people who pull you up | Only learning "consulting-specific stuff" |
| Good for people without purpose OR without courage | Doesn't lead to startup — actually pulls you AWAY |

**João's quote:** *"BCG is great if you either don't have purpose OR don't have courage. It's a low-hanging fruit with low opportunity cost... but it won't lead you to create a startup. It will pull you away from it."*

---

### What Startup Gives You

| Advantage | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| Learn by doing | You learn much more through experience |
| Uncontrolled environment | Forces you to actually learn |
| Find GOOD mentors | Direct, ongoing relationships with people who've done it |
| Start in a startup with a mission like yours | Get inside an ecosystem that exposes you to problems you care about (e.g., Sword Health) |

**João's quote:** *"A startup in a mission-aligned space lets you understand the real problems in areas you care about."*

---

### On the MBA Question

João asked: **"How long have you wanted to create something? If you want to build, why are you finding excuses to do a 2-year master's?"**

| MBA Reality | João's Take |
|-------------|-------------|
| €100K+/year to live there without earning | Is that worth it? |
| Network, ideas, learn company management | You can get this elsewhere |

### The Stamp Question

**The stamp is not worth it JUST for the stamp.** After 2 years you're a "BCGer" with a name and international network — but is that the fastest path to your goal?

## ⚠️ THE ACTUAL CONCLUSION: It's Not Either/Or

### João's Bias (Important Context)

João left BCG. Of course he's anti-BCG now. That's his lived experience. But his situation isn't my situation:

| João's Reality | My Reality |
|----------------|------------|
| He was already IN BCG and felt undervalued | I've never been IN BCG — I don't know yet |
| He has Augusta/VCs backing him | I don't (yet) |
| He made his choice already | I still have optionality |

**His advice is valid but biased.** He's rationalizing his own path.

---

### The Smarter Move: BOTH IN PARALLEL

The original strategy was NOT "BCG or startup" — it was **BOTH IN PARALLEL:**

| Do Now | Decide Later |
|--------|-------------|
| Work with Young-Wook → get paid, build real GTM experience | Wait to see if BCG accepts you |
| Apply to BCG → keep interviewing | If BCG says yes → compare offers with real data |
| Build real commercial track record | If BCG says no → you're already building anyway |

**This is risk-free optionality, not an either/or choice.**

---

### Why This Works

1. **You don't have to choose today** → Keep both paths alive until one closes or one clearly wins
2. **Let BCG decide for you** → Either they accept you (and you have a real offer to compare) or they reject you (and the decision is made)
3. **Build while waiting** → Young-Wook deal gives you income + track record regardless of BCG outcome
4. **Don't drop out of BCG process because João said it's not for builders** → He might be right, but get the offer first, then decide

---

### The Real Decision Framework

```
IF BCG accepts you:
    → Compare: BCG offer vs. what you've built with Young-Wook
    → Decide with REAL data, not hypotheticals
    → You can always say NO to BCG after getting in

IF BCG rejects you:
    → Decision made for you
    → You're already building anyway
    → No regret, no "what if"

EITHER WAY:
    → You're building NOW (Young-Wook, own projects)
    → You're not waiting for permission
    → You're not delaying
```
