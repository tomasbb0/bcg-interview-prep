That is my discipline. That is my function. That is my fire.

## 2. My Professional Journey: A Complete Timeline

*This timeline stitches together every scrap of biographical detail, from childhood to the present, forming the continuous narrative of my purpose.*

### 0 – 5 yrs | Crèche-king, then social reset
I arrived in the world brimming with *porquês* and devoured the picture books that crammed every shelf at home. By kindergarten I was the adored “crèche king,” forever performing jokes and small piano riffs for classmates. At age 5, however, my parents moved me to Salesianos one year later than the intake. I walked into a room whose social map was already drawn; popularity drained away overnight. A first ego-shock landed: status is fragile, grades might be the safest shield.

### 6 – 8 yrs | Top of every list, armour forged
Throughout 1.º ciclo I led the class in reading fluency, mental maths and science trivia. Teachers quoted me in staff meetings; peers copied homework. That recognition felt like compensation for the lost crèche spotlight—and it cemented an identity: excellence = safety.

### 9 – 12 yrs | Window-seat cosmologist & first jabs
2.º ciclo piled on harder algebra and verb conjugations; marks slid from stellar to “good, sometimes average.” Teasing about my “femininity” surfaced. Instead of playgrounds I gravitated to the window, tracing fractal branches and traffic-light rhythms with near-mystical awe. There I sketched my famous day-dream: hold twelve careers at once—engineer, architect, ski racer, astronaut, manager, musician, doctor, writer, philosopher, photographer, pilot and chef—convinced that mastering many levers will one day serve a larger mission.

### 13 – 15 yrs | Bullying crescendo, metaphysics & nocturnal notebooks
3.º ciclo was brutal. Ridicule became daily, especially as I secretly questioned my sexuality. Walking corridors, I studied classmates like clockwork mechanics, wondering whether cruelty is scripted. Evenings vanished into notebooks on determinism, solipsism and whether numbers exist independently of minds. Grades wobbled; ego stiffened into armour. I felt utterly alone but vowed: I’ll solve these cosmic riddles even if it kills me.

### 16 yrs | First classroom change & the “immortality blueprint”
Returning to Salesianos for 11.º ano—my first new cohort since primary school—rebooted social anxiety yet jolted fresh thinking. I declared a personal covenant: humanity can engineer immortality, maybe even exit this reality into a “higher level” one. If so, society itself must be rebuilt from scratch, starting small but dreaming universe-scale.

### 17 yrs | U.S. exchange — sexuality embraced, destiny super-charged
A year in an American high-school swapped Catholic corridors for rainbow clubs. I finally accepted my sexuality and felt what I labeled a “God-given mission”: make humanity live forever so science never runs out of time to answer every metaphysical question.

### 18 yrs | Academic resurrection & NOVA-SBE ticket
Back at Salesianos, I hauled Maths from 5/20 to 18/20 in the national exam—regaining top-student status—while drafting venture sketches on longevity and new societal models. NOVA-SBE accepted me.

### 18 – 22 yrs | NOVA-SBE — laboratory of social redesign
*   **Nova Tech Club:** Led events & coded a job-platform for low-education workers, testing tech as an equaliser.
*   **Nova Social Consulting:** Mapped 155 organisational pain points for an environmental NGO, learning diagnostic rigour.
*   **Nova Thirst Project:** Recruited 23 volunteers, raised €2,539 for clean-water wells.
*   **Portfolio Management Club & Awareness Club:** Sharpened data-storytelling and mass-engagement skills.
*   **WiSample & Jump Pre-Acceleration:** Turned abstract ideas into working MVPs.
*   **Lock-down evenings:** Produced the *Waterfall Approach*: accelerating automation will rip the wage-for-survival scaffold away, forcing civilisation toward a science-centric, longevity-focused order.

### 22 – 23 yrs | Closeted love & psychological collapse
A secret relationship oscillated between passion and panic, detonating derealisation episodes. Yet professional grind continued: I audited €40B in insurance assets at EY and propelled Amazon’s B2B launch for 2,300+ Portuguese firms, lifting territory output by 80%. Journals filled with existential OCD loops.

### Mid-2023 | Cambodia — altruism on fumes
I spent the summer tutoring under-privileged kids with Dream Big Cambodia, hoping to rekindle purpose. The work was meaningful, but burnout shadowed every lesson.

### Sep 2023 – Jan 2024 | Rotterdam — self-retooling boot-camp
Enrolled in the MSc in Strategic Management (GPA 8/10). Piled on LiveVersity micro-courses: Productivity & Time Management, Advanced Public Speaking, MS Excel. Launched “Students Meet Startups” (150 attendees) and dissected 20+ consulting cases for the Rotterdam Consulting Club—but existential fog remained.

### May – Aug 2024 | Google internship — technical apex, spiritual vacuum
Built three scalable dashboards saving €1.41M annually and briefed 200+ C-level clients. The achievement felt hollow against cosmic dread.

### Sep 2024 – Jan 2025 | Singapore & 10+-country trek — the great re-wire
Stationed in Singapore, I backpacked through more than ten Asian nations. The unguarded kindness of strangers—monks in Chiang Mai, hostel owners in Hanoi, surfers in Bali—steadied my spiralling thoughts. Deep dives into theology and simulation theory still triggered OCD loops, ADHD still scattered focus, yet one conclusion crystallised: happiness is rooted in love, and that love—felt even in absence—is undeniably real.
With that, the messianic “immortality crusade” felt like ego masquerading as altruism. I discarded it and adopted a new, humbler mission: mainstream mental-health literacy, ego-detection and evidence-based enlightenment. Societies of the future must normalise psychological safety.

### Nov 2024 – Present | Pairwire — sceptical execution dojo
Co-founded Pairwire, an AI due-diligence platform (2,154 leads engaged, pilots running). Pairwire was not designed to “save the world”; it was my personal laboratory for three reasons:
1.  **Structural-insight reps:** Building software that reveals hidden risks trains the muscles needed to expose survival-labour structures later.
2.  **Evidence discipline:** KPI-tight funnels and investor memos keep me strategically sceptical, guarding against uncritical spirituality.
3.  **Culture prototyping:** Weekly ego-check-ins, open dashboards and mental-health stipends let me test how compassion, transparency and high performance can co-habit.
Pairwire proved I can translate high-minded ideals into rigorous execution without losing empirical footing.

### Very late 2025 | Determinism epilogue
Reading neuro-philosophy on a night train, I realised free will might be an illusion—yet deliberate, compassionate actions still push causal chains. Maximising that ripple is the final synthesis.

### Current purpose (April 2025)
Enable humanity to leave survival labour behind and create evidence-based, mentally healthy societies—stretching both the intensity of happiness and the span of life—while ensuring ego-awareness and compassionate action anchor every layer of progress.
