# PITCH ASSETS: What You Built & Can Show

When pitching for your next Co-Founder role or VP Sales position, **show, don't tell.** You need to gather these "artifacts" from your time at Pairwire (scrubbing confidential data where necessary) to prove your competence.

## 1. The "Builder" Metrics
*   **Pipeline Growth:** "Built sales pipeline from 0 to [X] qualified leads in 10 months."
*   **Enterprise Penetration:** "Opened doors with Tier-1 clients (Enghouse, Mazars) without a marketing budget."
*   **Conversion:** "Managed end-to-end cycle from cold outreach to pilot negotiation."

## 2. The "Artifacts" (Gather these NOW)
*   **The Brand Book:** If you designed the brand, keep a PDF of the brand guidelines or visual identity. It proves you understand product marketing.
*   **The Sales Playbook:** Do you have a document outlining the sales process, scripts, or outreach strategy? Keep a sanitized version. It proves you can build systems.
*   **The Market Analysis:** Any research you did on competitors or market sizing. Proves strategic thinking.
*   **The "Stack" Diagram:** A simple visual of how you set up the ops (Notion -> Slack -> CRM). Proves operational competence.

## 3. The "Rolodex" (Your Network)
*   **Client Contacts:** Keep the names of the people you impressed at Enghouse, Mazars, etc. Connect with them on LinkedIn *now*.
    *   *Message:* "Hey [Name], just letting you know I'm moving on from Pairwire to pursue a new opportunity. Loved working with you and hope our paths cross again."
*   **Investors:** If you met any VCs or Angels, keep them warm. "I'm leaving Pairwire, but I'm working on something new."

## 4. The "Skill Stack" to List on LinkedIn
*   **0-to-1 Sales:** Cold outreach, pipeline architecture, CRM implementation.
*   **Partnerships:** Enterprise negotiation, pilot structuring.
*   **Operations:** Remote team setup, async workflows (Notion/Slack).
*   **Product Marketing:** Positioning, messaging, brand identity.
