# YOUNG-WOOK CALL — FEB 18, 2026

## QUICK CHEAT SHEET (Read in 2 min)

---

## 1. OPEN THE CALL (First 30 sec)

> "Young, really sorry again about the time mix-up — appreciate your flexibility. How are you doing?"

**Be warm, brief, then move on. Don't over-apologize.**

---

## 2. HIS UNANSWERED QUESTIONS (Answer these early)

### "How did you sell your product? Did you hire a developer?"

> "I build everything myself — I code, design, and ship. For the financial platform I sold this week, I built the full stack solo. No developer needed. That's actually one of my strengths — I can go from idea to production-ready product fast."

### "What position at Google?"

> "I'm in the process for a business/strategy role — I interned there before in Dublin and a VP I worked with referred me back. It's exciting but also a long process, you know how Google is!"

**Then flip it:** "That's amazing you're also talking to Google! How's that going for the medical AI role?"

### His app PrepGenius
> "I checked out PrepGenius — really cool concept! How has traction been? Are people using it?"

**This builds rapport and shows you care about his work.**

---

## 3. THE REAL AGENDA — What are you here for?

**Two possible collaboration angles:**

| Option                        | What it is                                      | Your interest level                                  |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **A) Mental Health Platform** | Co-build with him as healthtech/medical advisor | HIGH — Nova SBE sponsoring, you need a health expert |
| **B) His Startup GTM**        | Sales/growth work for his healthtech SaaS       | MEDIUM — only if terms are right                     |

**YOUR PRIORITY = Option A.** You have momentum, a sponsor, and need someone with his medical/healthtech expertise.

---

## 4. KEY TALKING POINTS (Mental Health Platform)

- **Nova SBE is sponsoring** → gives credibility + runway
- **You're building it** → you handle product/tech/business
- **You need a health expert** → he's a doctor + healthtech founder
- **His role could be:** Medical advisor, clinical validation, health AI input
- **Frame it as:** "I'd love your input on the clinical side — you understand patient needs better than anyone I know"

### Your Pitch (keep it casual):

> "The mental health platform is the thing I'm most passionate about right now. Nova SBE is backing it, I'm building the tech, but what I'm missing is someone who truly understands healthcare from the inside. That's you. I'm not asking you to quit anything — I'd love to explore if there's a way for you to be involved, even part-time or in an advisory capacity."

---

## 5. LISTEN FOR THESE SIGNALS

| He says...                          | It means...                            | You respond...                                                                    |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "I'm interested but busy"           | Open door, needs low-commitment option | "Even a few hours a month as advisor would be huge"                               |
| "What's the equity split?"          | He's seriously interested              | "Let's not rush that — let's first see if we work well together on a small scope" |
| "I want to focus on my startup"     | Not available right now                | "Totally get it — let's stay in touch and revisit when timing works"              |
| "What about working on MY startup?" | He wants to redirect to his GTM needs  | See Section 6 below                                                               |
| "Tell me more about the platform"   | Very interested                        | Share the vision, ask for his clinical input right there                          |

---

## 6. IF HE BRINGS UP HIS STARTUP (GTM work)

**Don't reject, but don't commit cheap either.**

> "I'm definitely open to exploring that too. Right now I'm juggling a few projects and my bandwidth is limited, but I'd love to understand where you are with sales and see if there's something that makes sense for both of us."

**If he pushes for pricing:** Use the old framework — €1,500/month trial, 50/50 advance. But DON'T go there unless he initiates.

---

## 7. HOW TO CLOSE THE CALL (Last 2 min)

**If mental health collab seems promising:**

> "This was great. How about we both think about what your involvement could look like, and I'll send you a short outline this week?"

**If he's more interested in his own startup:**

> "Let me think about my availability and send you a proposal for what I could realistically do."

**If unclear:**

> "Really glad we reconnected. Let's keep the dialogue going — I'll follow up with some thoughts this week."

---

## 8. GOLDEN RULES FOR THIS CALL

1. **YOU lead the conversation** — don't let it drift into small talk for 30 min
2. **Ask more than you tell** — "Where's your head at?" / "What are you most excited about?"
3. **Don't commit to anything on the call** — "Let me put together something concrete and send it over"
4. **Show momentum** — you have projects, Google, BCG, Nova SBE. You're in demand.
5. **Be genuine** — he's smart, don't oversell. Authenticity > persuasion.
6. **Time-box it** — aim for 30-40 min max. End on energy, not exhaustion.
