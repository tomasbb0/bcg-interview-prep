## 2. Legal Definitions

<a id="a-introduction-legal-definitions"></a>
### a. Introduction

This section establishes the legal framework under Portuguese law for recognizing an individual as a Co-Founder (*Sócio Fundador*). It defines the statutory requirements for partnership status and explains the legal doctrines that protect individuals who have been treated as partners in substance, regardless of formal contractual designations.

---

<a id="b-legal-equivalence-co-founder--sócio-fundador"></a>
### b. Legal Equivalence: "Co-Founder" = "Sócio Fundador"

The term "Co-Founder" is the English commercial equivalent of the Portuguese legal term **"Sócio Fundador"**.

This equivalence is established by the **Código das Sociedades Comerciais (CSC)**, which explicitly designates the initial members of a company as "sócios fundadores".

> **Source:** Código das Sociedades Comerciais (CSC)
> **Article:** Artigo 9.º (Elementos do contrato)
> **Quote:** "1 - Do contrato de qualquer tipo de sociedade devem constar: a) Os nomes ou firmas de todos os **sócios fundadores** [...]"
> **Link:** [Procuradoria-Geral Distrital de Lisboa - CSC](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?nid=524&tabela=leis)

Therefore, to claim the title of "Co-Founder" is to claim the legal status of a **Partner ("Sócio")** who participated in the foundation of the entity.

> **Simple Translation:** Claiming the title of "Co-Founder" is legally equivalent to claiming the status of a "Partner". The law does not distinguish between the English business title and the Portuguese legal status.

---

<a id="c-statutory-definition-of-a-partner-sócio"></a>
### c. Statutory Definition of a Partner ("Sócio")

As the Claimant was not included in the formal written contract, his status must be proven as a **De Facto Partner** (*Sócio de Facto*). The definition of this relationship is governed by the **Código Civil (Civil Code)**.

> **Source:** Código Civil (CC)
> **Article:** Artigo 980.º (Noção)
> **Quote:** "Contrato de sociedade é aquele em que duas ou mais pessoas se obrigam a **contribuir com bens ou serviços** para o **exercício em comum de certa actividade económica**, que não seja de mera fruição, a fim de **repartirem os lucros**."
> **Link:** [Procuradoria-Geral Distrital de Lisboa - CC](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?nid=775&tabela=leis)

> **Simple Translation:** A formal written contract is not required to establish partnership status. If an individual contributes work, shares in decision-making, and expects profits, the law automatically defines the relationship as a partnership.

---

<a id="d-the-three-legal-criteria-framework-for-analysis"></a>
### d. The Three Legal Criteria (Framework for Analysis)

Based on the statutory definition in Article 980 of the Civil Code, the legal status of a Co-Founder is established by the fulfillment of three mandatory criteria. These criteria will form the structure of the evidentiary analysis.

#### Criterion I: Contribution ("Contribuir com bens ou serviços")
The law mandates a binding obligation to contribute assets or services to the partnership. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 980 - "...obrigam a contribuir com bens ou serviços...")**.
*   *Legal Test:* Whether the individual provided essential services or assets to the creation or operation of the entity.

#### Criterion II: Joint Exercise of Activity ("Exercício em comum")
The law requires the activity to be exercised in common, implying shared decision-making and strategic control, distinct from a subordinate employee relationship. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 980 - "...para o exercício em comum de certa actividade económica...")**.
*   *Legal Test:* Whether the individual participated in strategic decisions and management ("exercício em comum") rather than merely executing orders.

#### Criterion III: Profit Sharing ("Repartirem os lucros")
The law requires an intention to share in the results (profits) of the activity, which distinguishes a partner from an employee (who receives a fixed salary) or a service provider. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 980 - "...a fim de repartirem os lucros.")**.
*   *Legal Test:* Whether there was an agreement or legitimate expectation to share in the equity or profits of the company.

> **Simple Translation:** To establish this status, three elements must be proven: (1) Contribution of work or assets, (2) Participation in strategic decision-making (distinct from employee tasks), and (3) Agreement or expectation to share in profits/equity.

<a id="e-de-facto-partnership--late-entry-sócio-de-facto"></a>
### e. De Facto Partnership & Late Entry ("Sócio de Facto")
*Applicable to scenarios involving entry subsequent to incorporation or concealment under a "Consulting Agreement".*

**The "Late Co-Founder" Doctrine:**
The status of a partner is not precluded by an individual's entry into the company subsequent to its formal incorporation, nor by the existence of a nominal "Consulting Agreement". Portuguese law mandates an analysis of the *substance* of the relationship over its formal designation. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 980 - "Contrato de sociedade é aquele em que duas ou mais pessoas se obrigam a contribuir... para o exercício em comum... a fim de repartirem os lucros."; Código das Sociedades Comerciais, Art. 36, nº 1 - "...são aplicáveis às relações estabelecidas entre eles... as disposições sobre sociedades civis.")**.

**1. Primacy of Reality (Substance Over Form):**
Where an individual contributes labor or capital and shares in the risks and profits with the intent of partnership (*affectio societatis*), the legal status of Partner (*Sócio de Facto*) is established, irrespective of the nomenclature applied to the contract. **(Source: Acórdão do STJ de 04-11-2004, Proc. 04B3099 - "A substância do acordo prevalece sobre a forma"; Acórdão do STJ de 07-07-2010 - "A qualificação jurídica do contrato não depende do *nomen iuris* atribuído pelas partes, mas sim do conteúdo real da relação.")**. Consequently, a "Consulting Agreement" that conceals a true partnership relationship is null and void as a simulation, and the underlying partnership agreement prevails. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 240, nº 2 - "Se, por acordo... divergirem da sua vontade real... o negócio é nulo por simulação."; Código Civil, Art. 236, nº 1 - "A declaração negocial vale com o sentido que um declaratário normal... possa deduzir do comportamento do declarante.")**.

**2. Abuse of Right & Illegality of Forced Denial:**
*   **Prohibition of Contradictory Behavior (*Venire Contra Factum Proprium*):** It is unlawful for a corporate entity to treat an individual as a partner—accepting labor and promising equity—and subsequently deny such status based on a lack of formality. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 334 - "É ilegítimo o exercício de um direito, quando o titular exceda manifestamente os limites impostos pela boa fé...")**. Such conduct constitutes *Venire Contra Factum Proprium*, which is strictly prohibited when it betrays the legitimate trust created in the counterparty. **(Source: Acórdão do STJ de 04-11-2004 - "A actuação de se negar... integraria também abuso de direito")**.
*   **Nullity of Waiver of Professional Status:** Any agreement or demand requiring an individual to publicly deny their historical status as a Co-Founder is legally void. The right to one's professional identity and reputation is a personality right that cannot be voluntarily renounced if such renunciation is contrary to truth and public order. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 81, nº 1 - "Toda a limitação voluntária ao exercício dos direitos de personalidade é nula, se for contrária aos princípios da ordem pública."; Código Civil, Art. 72, nº 1 - "Toda a pessoa tem direito a usar o seu nome... no exercício de uma actividade profissional...")**.
*   **Unenforceability of False Declarations:** Consequently, even if the Claimant were to sign a document stating they were "not a Co-Founder", such a clause would be null and unenforceable for violating public order by compelling a falsehood. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 280, nº 1 - "É nulo o negócio jurídico cujo objecto seja... contrário à ordem pública.")**.

**3. Liability & Rights:**
In the context of an irregular or de facto partnership, the relations between the parties are governed by the agreement and general partnership rules, entitling the partner to a share of the value created. **(Source: Código das Sociedades Comerciais, Art. 36, nº 1 - "...são aplicáveis às relações estabelecidas entre eles... as disposições sobre sociedades civis.")**. The retroactive removal of a partner from the company's history does not extinguish the right to compensation for the accrued value. **(Source: Código Civil, Art. 473 - "Aquele que, sem causa justificativa, enriquecer à custa de outrem é obrigado a restituir...")**.

> **Simple Translation:** The title of the contract ("Consulting Agreement") is irrelevant if the reality was a partnership. If the substantive relationship was that of a partner, the "Consulting" contract is void. Furthermore, any demand for an individual to deny their history is legally void, as it violates fundamental personality rights.

<a id="f-conclusion"></a>
### f. Conclusion

The legal framework is unequivocal: **Substance trumps Form.**

1.  **Status:** If the evidentiary analysis confirms the fulfillment of the three criteria (Contribution, Joint Activity, Profit Sharing), the Claimant meets the statutory definition of a **Partner (*Sócio*)** under Art. 980 CC.
2.  **Invalidity of Contract:** If the substantive relationship is proven to be a partnership, the "Consulting Agreement" constitutes a **simulation** (Art. 240 CC) designed to conceal the true legal nature of the bond.
3.  **Illegality of Denial:** Any attempt to force the Claimant to deny this status would constitute an **Abuse of Right** (Art. 334 CC) and a violation of **Personality Rights** (Art. 81 CC).

> **Simple Translation:** If the facts show that the individual acted as a partner, the law defines them as such. In that case, the "Consulting" contract would be invalid for hiding the truth. Furthermore, legally, no one can be forced to deny their true professional history.

<a id="c-analysis-of-exit-agreement-clause-7-correction-of-public-representations"></a>
### c. Analysis of Exit Agreement Clause 7 ("Correction of Public Representations")

The Company's proposed Exit Agreement contains a clause (Section 7) demanding the Claimant:
- Remove any reference to "Co-Founder," "Founder," "Partner," or any similar title from LinkedIn and all other platforms;
- Change the engagement start date to January 2025 and end date to November 2025;
- Complete these changes within 48 hours of signing;
- Provide written evidence (screenshots) of all corrections.

**This clause is illegal and void for the following reasons:**

#### 1. Violation of Personality Rights (Art. 70, 72, 81 CC)
The right to one's professional identity is a **personality right** (*direito de personalidade*) protected under Portuguese law. **(Source: Art. 72, nº 1 CC - "Toda a pessoa tem direito a usar o seu nome... no exercício de uma actividade profissional...")**. Any voluntary limitation of this right is **null and void** if contrary to public order. **(Source: Art. 81, nº 1 CC - "Toda a limitação voluntária ao exercício dos direitos de personalidade é nula, se for contrária aos princípios da ordem pública.")**.

#### 2. Nullity for Violation of Public Order (Art. 280 CC)
A contract clause that compels a party to make **false public statements** is contrary to public order and therefore void. **(Source: Art. 280, nº 1 CC - "É nulo o negócio jurídico cujo objecto seja... contrário à ordem pública.")**. The clause demands:
- Denial of the Claimant's true role (Co-Founder);
- Falsification of dates (November 2025 instead of the actual end date of December 2025).

#### 3. Abuse of Right (Art. 334 CC)
Demanding the erasure of the Claimant's professional history, after having treated the Claimant as a partner throughout the engagement, constitutes *Venire Contra Factum Proprium*. **(Source: Art. 334 CC - "É ilegítimo o exercício de um direito, quando o titular exceda manifestamente os limites impostos pela boa fé...")**.

#### 4. Potential Criminal Liability: Coercion (Art. 154 Código Penal)
If the Company conditioned payment or other benefits on signing this clause, this may constitute **coercion** (*coação*). **(Source: Código Penal, Art. 154 - "Quem... por meio de violência ou de ameaça com mal importante, constranger outra pessoa a uma acção, omissão ou a suportar uma actividade é punido com pena de prisão até 3 anos...")**.
